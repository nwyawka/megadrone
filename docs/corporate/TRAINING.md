## 1. Training Overview

### 1.1 Training Philosophy

JP Security employs a four-tier training model ensuring all personnel are prepared for their roles at corporate, position, mission, and individual levels.

```
TRAINING HIERARCHY

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        CORPORATE TRAINING                                    │
│                   (All personnel, company-wide)                              │
│  Company mission, values, policies, legal, safety, OPSEC                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                    │
                                    ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        POSITION TRAINING                                     │
│                    (Role-specific knowledge)                                 │
│  Operations, Intel, Maritime, Business - job duties and skills              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                    │
                                    ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        MISSION TRAINING                                      │
│                 (Deployment and operation specific)                          │
│  Area familiarization, tactics, procedures, rehearsals                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                    │
                                    ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                       INDIVIDUAL TRAINING                                    │
│              (Personal readiness and certifications)                         │
│  Certifications, physical fitness, personal equipment, skills maintenance   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 1.2 Training Objectives

| Level | Objective |
|-------|-----------|
| **Corporate** | Ensure all personnel understand company mission, policies, and culture |
| **Position** | Prepare personnel for specific job responsibilities |
| **Mission** | Ready teams for specific operational deployments |
| **Individual** | Maintain personal readiness and required certifications |

## 2. Corporate Training

### 2.1 Purpose

Corporate training ensures all JP Security personnel, regardless of role, understand the company's mission, values, legal framework, and operational security requirements.

**Required For:** All personnel (employees, contractors, advisors)
**Frequency:** Initial onboarding + annual refresher

### 2.2 Corporate Training Modules

#### Module C-1: Company Overview
| Topic | Duration | Delivery |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Company history and founding | 30 min | Briefing |
| Mission statement and strategic intent | 30 min | Briefing |
| Letter of Marque vision | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Organizational structure | 30 min | Briefing |
| Team introductions | 30 min | In-person |

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand JP Security's primary mission (Letter of Marque)
- Understand interim operations model (government contracts)
- Know organizational structure and key personnel
- Understand company values and culture

#### Module C-2: Legal and Compliance
| Topic | Duration | Delivery |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Legal framework (current authority) | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Letter of Marque legal basis | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Rules of engagement (pre-LOM) | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Liability and insurance | 30 min | Briefing |
| Export control (ITAR) awareness | 30 min | Briefing |
| Regulatory compliance (FAA, FCC, USCG) | 1 hr | Briefing |

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand current legal authorities and limitations
- Know what actions are authorized vs prohibited
- Understand liability exposure and protections
- Recognize ITAR-controlled information

#### Module C-3: Operational Security (OPSEC)
| Topic | Duration | Delivery |
|-------|----------|----------|
| OPSEC principles | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Information classification | 30 min | Briefing |
| Communications security | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Social media policy | 30 min | Briefing |
| Physical security | 30 min | Briefing |
| Counterintelligence awareness | 30 min | Briefing |

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand critical information and indicators
- Apply appropriate classification to information
- Use secure communications methods
- Recognize social engineering attempts

#### Module C-4: Safety
| Topic | Duration | Delivery |
|-------|----------|----------|
| General safety policies | 30 min | Briefing |
| Emergency procedures | 1 hr | Practical |
| First aid basics | 2 hr | Practical |
| Maritime safety | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Aviation safety (drones) | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Hazardous materials (batteries, fuel) | 30 min | Briefing |

**Learning Objectives:**
- Know emergency procedures and contacts
- Perform basic first aid
- Recognize and mitigate safety hazards
- Handle hazardous materials properly

#### Module C-5: Ethics and Conduct
| Topic | Duration | Delivery |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Code of conduct | 30 min | Briefing |
| Conflict of interest | 30 min | Briefing |
| Use of force philosophy | 1 hr | Briefing |
| Interaction with authorities | 30 min | Briefing |
| Reporting requirements | 30 min | Briefing |

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand expected standards of conduct
- Know when and how to use force (defense only, pre-LOM)
- Properly interact with law enforcement and authorities
- Report incidents through proper channels

### 2.3 Corporate Training Evaluation

| Method | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| Written quiz (Modules C-1, C-2, C-3) | 80% minimum |
| Practical demonstration (C-4 Safety) | Pass/Fail |
| Acknowledgment signature | Required |

## 3. Position Training

### 3.1 Purpose

Position training prepares personnel for their specific job responsibilities within JP Security.

**Required For:** All personnel, based on assigned role
**Frequency:** Initial assignment + when duties change

### 3.2 Position Categories

| Position | Primary Responsibilities | Training Track |
|----------|-------------------------|----------------|
| **Operations Lead** | Field operations, team leadership | P-OPS |
| **Drone Operator** | Platform operation, maintenance | P-DRONE |
| **Boat Operator** | Maritime operations, vessel handling | P-MARITIME |
| **Analyst** | Intelligence analysis, reporting | P-INTEL |
| **Business/Admin** | Contracts, finance, administration | P-ADMIN |

### 3.3 Position Training Tracks

#### Track P-OPS: Operations Lead
| Module | Topic | Duration |
|--------|-------|----------|
| P-OPS-1 | Leadership and team management | 4 hr |
| P-OPS-2 | Operational planning | 4 hr |
| P-OPS-3 | CONOPS overview | 2 hr |
| P-OPS-4 | Decision-making under pressure | 2 hr |
| P-OPS-5 | Incident command | 2 hr |
| P-OPS-6 | Coordination with authorities | 2 hr |

**Prerequisites:** Complete Corporate Training, prior operational experience
**Evaluation:** Scenario-based assessment, written exam

#### Track P-DRONE: Drone Operator
| Module | Topic | Duration |
|--------|-------|----------|
| P-DRONE-1 | Platform familiarization (VA-) | 8 hr |
| P-DRONE-2 | ArduPilot/ground station operation | 8 hr |
| P-DRONE-3 | Pre-flight/post-flight procedures | 4 hr |
| P-DRONE-4 | Normal operations | 8 hr |
| P-DRONE-5 | Emergency procedures | 4 hr |
| P-DRONE-6 | Maintenance and repair | 8 hr |
| P-DRONE-7 | Camera/sensor operation | 4 hr |
| P-DRONE-8 | Maritime operations (from boat) | 4 hr |

**Prerequisites:** FAA Part 107 certification, Corporate Training
**Evaluation:** Flight check (minimum 5 hours logged), written exam

**Platform-Specific Sub-Modules:**
| Platform | Additional Training |
|----------|---------------------|
| VA-1/VA-2 | Fixed-wing launch/recovery, cruise ops |
| VA-4 | VTOL transition, long-endurance ops |
| VA-5R | Quad operations, close-range ISR |
| VSS-1I | ROV piloting (tethered), inspection |

#### Track P-MARITIME: Boat Operator
| Module | Topic | Duration |
|--------|-------|----------|
| P-MAR-1 | Vessel familiarization | 4 hr |
| P-MAR-2 | Navigation and seamanship | 8 hr |
| P-MAR-3 | Rules of the road (COLREGS) | 4 hr |
| P-MAR-4 | Weather and sea state | 4 hr |
| P-MAR-5 | Communications (VHF, distress) | 2 hr |
| P-MAR-6 | Drone launch/recovery from vessel | 4 hr |
| P-MAR-7 | Emergency procedures (man overboard, fire) | 4 hr |

#### Track P-INTEL: Analyst

| P-INTEL-1 | Intelligence fundamentals | 4 hr |
| P-INTEL-2 | Collection management | 4 hr |
| P-INTEL-3 | Analysis techniques | 8 hr |
| P-INTEL-4 | Reporting and dissemination | 4 hr |
| P-INTEL-5 | AIS and vessel databases | 4 hr |
| P-INTEL-6 | Imagery analysis | 8 hr |
| P-INTEL-7 | Pattern of life analysis | 4 hr |
| P-INTEL-8 | Target packages | 4 hr |

**Prerequisites:** Corporate Training, security clearance (if applicable)
**Evaluation:** Analysis exercise, written exam

#### Track P-ADMIN: Business/Administration
| Module | Topic | Duration |
|--------|-------|----------|
| P-ADMIN-1 | Government contracting basics | 4 hr |
| P-ADMIN-2 | Financial management | 4 hr |
| P-ADMIN-3 | Contract administration | 4 hr |
| P-ADMIN-4 | Regulatory compliance | 2 hr |

**Evaluation:** Written exam

## 4. Mission Training

### 4.1 Purpose

Mission training prepares teams for specific operational deployments, ensuring familiarity with the area, mission objectives, and coordinated procedures.

**Required For:** All personnel deploying on a specific mission
**Frequency:** Prior to each deployment

### 4.2 Mission Training Components

```
MISSION TRAINING SEQUENCE

T-14 days          T-7 days           T-3 days           T-0
    │                  │                  │                │
    ▼                  ▼                  ▼                ▼
┌─────────┐      ┌─────────┐      ┌─────────┐      ┌─────────┐
│ Mission │      │  Area   │      │  Team   │      │ Deploy  │
│ Brief   │─────►│ Famil   │─────►│Rehearsal│─────►│         │
└─────────┘      └─────────┘      └─────────┘      └─────────┘
```

#### M-1: Mission Brief
| Topic | Duration | Lead |
|-------|----------|------|
| Mission objectives | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Area of operations | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Threat assessment | 30 min | Intel |
| Platform assignments | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Timeline and schedule | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Communications plan | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Coordination with authorities | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Logistics | 30 min | Operations Lead |
| Questions and contingencies | 30 min | All |

#### M-2: Area Familiarization
| Topic | Duration | Method |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Geographic orientation | 1 hr | Maps, charts, imagery |
| Local facilities and resources | 30 min | Briefing |
| Weather patterns | 30 min | Briefing |
| Traffic patterns (vessel, air) | 1 hr | AIS data, briefing |
| Points of interest | 30 min | Briefing |
| Emergency facilities (hospitals, USCG) | 30 min | Briefing |
| Local contacts and liaisons | 30 min | Briefing |

**Example: Key West Area Familiarization**
| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| AO boundaries | Florida Straits, defined patrol zones |
| Marina locations | Boza's contacts, staging area |
| USCG Sector | Key West contact procedures |
| Airspace | Part 107 requirements, restricted areas |
| Sea conditions | Typical wind, wave, current |

#### M-3: Team Rehearsal
| Activity | Duration | Participants |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Communications check | 30 min | All |
| Platform pre-flight/inspection | 2 hr | Operators |
| Launch/recovery drill | 2 hr | Operators, boat crew |
| Emergency procedure walkthrough | 1 hr | All |
| Coordination exercise (tabletop) | 2 hr | All |
| Full mission rehearsal | 4 hr | All |

### 4.3 Mission-Specific Checklists

**Pre-Deployment Checklist:**
- [ ] All personnel completed mission brief
- [ ] Area familiarization complete
- [ ] Team rehearsal conducted
- [ ] Equipment inspected and loaded
- [ ] Communications tested
- [ ] Weather checked and acceptable
- [ ] Authorities notified (if required)
- [ ] Emergency contacts verified

**Post-Mission Debrief:**
- [ ] Mission objectives achieved?
- [ ] What went well?
- [ ] What could be improved?
- [ ] Equipment issues?
- [ ] Lessons learned documented

## 5. Individual Training

### 5.1 Purpose

Individual training ensures each team member maintains personal readiness, required certifications, and necessary skills for their role.

**Required For:** All personnel
**Frequency:** Ongoing, per certification requirements

### 5.2 Certification Requirements

#### Mandatory Certifications (All Field Personnel)
| Certification | Requirement | Renewal |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| First Aid/CPR | Current certification | 2 years |
| Water Safety | Basic swimming proficiency | One-time |

#### Role-Specific Certifications

**Drone Operators:**
| Certification | Requirement | Renewal |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| FAA Part 107 | Required | 2 years (recurrent) |
| Part 107 Waiver | As needed for operations | Per waiver terms |

**Boat Operators:**
| Certification | Requirement | Renewal |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Florida Boater Safety | Required | One-time |
| USCG OUPV License | Required for paid operations | 5 years |
| Ship Radio Operator | VS-1 operations | None |

**Communications:**
| Certification | Requirement | Renewal |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| FCC GMRS License | Team comms | 10 years |
| FCC Ham License | Emergency backup | 10 years |

**Firearms (Optional):**
| Certification | Requirement | Renewal |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Florida CWL | Personal carry | 7 years |
| Firearms proficiency | If carrying | Annual |

### 5.3 Proficiency Requirements

**Drone Operators:**
| Skill | Minimum Requirement | Documentation |
|-------|---------------------|---------------|
| Flight hours | 5 hours/quarter | Flight log |
| Platform types | Current on assigned platforms | Check ride |
| Emergency procedures | Annual demonstration | Evaluation form |

**Boat Operators:**
| Skill | Minimum Requirement | Documentation |
|-------|---------------------|---------------|
| Underway hours | 10 hours/quarter | Vessel log |
| Drone launch/recovery | 3 evolutions/quarter | Log |
| Emergency drills | Quarterly | Drill record |

**Analysts:**
| Skill | Minimum Requirement | Documentation |
|-------|---------------------|---------------|
| Analysis products | 1 product/month | Review |
| Database proficiency | Current | Evaluation |
| Continuing education | 4 hours/quarter | Training record |

### 5.4 Physical Readiness

**Recommended Standards:**
| Requirement | Standard | Frequency |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Swimming | 100m unassisted | Annual |
| Water treading | 10 minutes | Annual |
| General fitness | Self-assessed | Ongoing |

**Note:** Physical standards are recommended, not mandatory. Personnel should maintain fitness appropriate for their role.

### 5.5 Personal Equipment

**Required Personal Kit:**
| Item | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Personal protective equipment (PPE) | Individual |
| Appropriate clothing for operations | Individual |
| Personal communication device | Individual |
| Personal first aid kit | Individual |
| Required documents (licenses, IDs) | Individual |

**Optional Personal Equipment:**
| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Personal firearm (if CWL) | Brown or individual |
| Personal tools | As appropriate |
| Specialized gear | Per role |

### 5.6 Skill Maintenance

**Self-Study Resources:**
- ArduPilot documentation and forums
- FAA resources and updates
- USCG Navigation Rules
- Industry publications

**Recommended Training:**
- Advanced first aid / wilderness first aid
- SCUBA certification (for VSS operations)
- Advanced navigation courses
- Radio operator courses
