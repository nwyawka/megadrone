## Task Details

### 1. Define VA- platform build priorities and sequence
Determine which air platforms to build first (VA-0 through VA-5), establish build order based on dependencies and operational needs. Consider skills progression, component commonality, and budget.

### 2. Define VS-1D build timeline and milestones
Create development schedule for VS-1D (Diesel + Electric) surface vessel. Include hull construction, propulsion integration, electronics, and sea trials. VS-1D is the starting point for surface platforms.

### 3. Define VSS-1I build timeline and milestones
Create development schedule for VSS-1I (Inspector) submersible. Starting point for subsurface platforms. Include frame construction, thruster integration, pressure testing, and pool/open water trials.

### 4. Create component procurement list for priority platforms
Identify long-lead items and create procurement schedule. Include motors, batteries, electronics, hull materials, sensors for VA-, VS-, and VSS- platforms selected for initial build.

### 5. Develop 2026 operational schedule
Create calendar of operational activities for 2026. Include platform testing windows, deployment dates, Gulf of Mexico operations, training events, and maintenance periods.

### 6. Identify deployment locations and requirements
Define shore facilities, launch sites, and operational areas. Include Gulf of Mexico patrol zones for VS-1, air operations areas for VA- platforms, and recovery/maintenance locations.

### 7. Define training requirements and schedule
Identify training needs for operators across VA-, VS-, and VSS- platforms. Include pilot training, autopilot operation, maintenance training, and emergency procedures.

### 8. Complete government contracting registration (SAM.gov, CAGE code)
Register in System for Award Management (SAM.gov), obtain CAGE code, DUNS number. Required for federal contracting. Verify entity information and complete all required certifications.

### 9. Identify required certifications and licenses
Research and list all required certifications: FAA Part 107, USCG regulations for USVs, maritime licenses, radio licenses (FCC), export control (ITAR) considerations for VA-5S. Create compliance roadmap.

### 10. Identify target government agencies and contract vehicles
Research potential government customers: CBP, USCG, DOD, DHS. Identify relevant contract vehicles (GSA Schedule, GWACs, BPAs). Map capabilities to agency needs.

### 11. Develop proposal strategy and capability brief
Create capability brief/pitch deck for government customers. Develop proposal templates, past performance documentation, and pricing strategy for drone services.

### 12. Introduce Waller to the company
Bring Waller into the organization. Brief on company mission, current status, platform development, and role expectations.

### 13. Roll out company to full team
Expand awareness to rest of team beyond core four (O'Neil, Brown, Boza, Devlin). Include Cieglo, Hartley, and others. Full briefing on mission, platforms, roles.

### 14. Plan and execute first corporate retreat
Organize first corporate retreat in May. Agenda: team building, strategic planning, role assignments, operational planning, platform demos if ready.

### 15. Engage investors - funding round planning
Identify potential investors, prepare pitch deck, financial projections, and funding requirements. Determine funding amount needed and use of funds.

### 16. Brief advisors on company status and plans
Update advisory board on company progress, platform development, business strategy. Seek guidance on key decisions.

### 17. Assess team experience gaps and hiring needs
Map current team skills against needs. Identify gaps in: engineering, operations, business development, legal, finance, intel. Determine hire vs. contractor vs. advisor for each gap.

### 18. Develop operational deployment plan
Create detailed plan for field deployments: locations, schedules, logistics, equipment transport, personnel rotations, communications, and support requirements.

### 19. Define job duties and role assignments
Create job descriptions and assign responsibilities for each team member. Include: leadership, platform development, operations, business development, intel, logistics.

### 20. Develop intel network and information sharing framework
Design intel collection, analysis, and dissemination processes. Identify sources, establish relationships, define reporting formats, and data security protocols.

### 21. Secure workshop/build facility
Identify and secure workspace for platform construction. Requirements: adequate space for 7m hull, power, ventilation for composites, tool storage, security.

### 22. Secure shore site for VS-1 operations
Identify launch/recovery site for VS-1 surface vessel. Requirements: boat ramp or dock access, staging area, fuel storage, proximity to Gulf operations area.

### 23. Set up IT/comms infrastructure
Establish IT infrastructure: Starlink accounts for platforms, secure communications (encrypted messaging), file servers/cloud storage, network security, backup systems.

### 24. Finalize entity structure (LLC/Corp)
Confirm legal entity structure for JP Security. Review LLC vs. Corp implications for contracting, liability, taxes. File any necessary amendments or formations.

### 25. Obtain insurance coverage
Secure insurance policies: general liability, equipment/hull coverage, maritime operations, professional liability, workers comp. Get quotes and bind coverage.

### 26. Finalize operating agreement and equity structure
Complete operating agreement defining ownership, equity splits, voting rights, profit distribution, roles. Get legal review and all member signatures.

### 27. Complete ITAR/export control review
Review ITAR and export control implications for VA-5S strike platform and other systems. Determine registration requirements, compliance procedures, and restrictions.

### 28. Define 2026 budget
Create detailed budget for 2026: platform development costs, operations, personnel, facilities, marketing, legal, contingency. Align with funding sources.

### 29. Set up accounting and bookkeeping
Establish accounting system: select software (QuickBooks, etc.), chart of accounts, expense tracking, invoicing, payroll setup. Consider CPA/bookkeeper hire.

### 30. Set up bank accounts and financial infrastructure
Open business bank accounts: operating account, savings/reserve, credit card. Set up payment processing, expense management, and financial controls.

### 31. Develop safety protocols and SOPs
Create standard operating procedures for: platform operations, field deployments, emergency response, equipment handling, hazardous materials (batteries, fuel, composites).

### 32. Develop OPSEC plan
Create operations security plan: information classification, need-to-know protocols, secure communications, physical security, social media policy, visitor procedures.

### 33. Complete background checks and clearances for team
Initiate background checks for all team members. Determine if security clearances needed for government work. Identify clearance sponsor if required.

### 34. Update website and public presence
Update JP Security website with capabilities, services offered. Consider what to make public vs. keep confidential. Professional imagery, contact information.

### 35. Identify trade shows and conferences to attend
Research relevant 2026 events: defense conferences, maritime security, drone industry. Prioritize for networking, business development, and market intelligence.

### 36. Establish vendor relationships
Identify and establish relationships with key suppliers: Blue Robotics, motor/ESC vendors, battery suppliers, composite materials, electronics. Negotiate pricing, lead times.

### 37. Identify testing facilities and ranges
Find locations for platform testing: airfields for VA- testing, water access for VS-1 trials, pool/open water for VSS-1. Consider FAA airspace, permits needed.
